Why it works

Designed with therapists, powered by science

Every part of the game maps to something that improves speech-sound outcomes.

🔁

Massed practice

Research shows more correct productions per session drives faster progress. A live trial counter pushes towards 50–100 reps without it feeling like work.

👂

Model & self-monitor

Every word is spoken aloud as a model. Children record their own attempt and play it back, building the self-monitoring that carries over to real speech.

👄

Placement cues

Each sound shows a kid-friendly mouth cue ("smile, teeth together, ssss") so children know how to make the sound, not just that they got it wrong.

How a game works

1 · Set the target

Pick the sound (S, R, L, SH, CH, K, G, F, TH), the position in the word, and the level. The therapist stays in control.

2 · Practise & record

The child hears the word, sees the picture, says it, and records themselves to compare against the model.

3 · You judge it

You tap "Got it", "Almost" or "Show me" — honest, clinician-led scoring, not unreliable auto-detection.

4 · Collect the animal

Correct productions earn a safari animal and move the explorer to the next scene. Progress they can see.

At the end you get a session summary — total productions and accuracy — ready to drop into your notes.
